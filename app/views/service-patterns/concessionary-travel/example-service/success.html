Where and when your pass is valid

  • on all local bus services in England
  • 9:30am to 11pm weekdays
  • anytime Saturdays, Sundays and bank holidays
Find out about local bus services in England

The pass can be used on many bus services throughout England, as part of the English national concessionary travel scheme (ENCTS). Local buses may include some tour buses and long-distance coach services that are registered as local bus services.

They don’t usually include sightseeing tours, other tourist related services, or services where the fare includes payment for another service, for example car parking in the case of Park-and-Ride.

National coach services are not generally included. National Express offers a fare reduction card to people over 60. This is different from the older person's bus pass.

Check with the bus operator if in doubt.

Using your pass

  • bring it with you when you travel by bus
  • show it to the bus driver, or tap it on the card reader
  • don't share your pass
  • don't keep more than 1 pass at a time
  • renew your pass when it expires in 5 years – you've signed up to receive a text and email when it's time to renew your pass

Using your GOV.UK Verify account

You can use your GOV.UK Verify account for other government services including:

  • report a medical condition that affects your driving
  • complete your claim or access your Universal Credit account
  • complete your Self Assessment
